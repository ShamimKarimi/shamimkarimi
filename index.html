Kiddo

Educational Application

Overview

Android application
Team of 2
Designed and implemented for Project Design course (Paris Sud University - 2018)

Objectives

When we were kids, we used to play outside all day and do all kinds of social and physical games with friends. We used to use our hands and make crafts, touch the soil in the garden, and have a fun time helping mom set the table. We did not need expensive toys to feel like we are queens and kings of our fortress made out of pillows.
But things have changed. Technology’s impact on our daily lives cannot be denied. Kids nowadays learn how to use an iPad at an early age. Although an iPad could be used as an amazing educational tool, it can keep the child from developing other necessary skills like sensory-motor skills and social interactions. As parents and tutors, it is our responsibility to control the balance, rather than using digital devices as babysitters.
Statistics show that physical, psychological, and behavior disorders are increased due to the overuse of technology in developing children. Interfering with sleep and schoolwork, cutting into family time, causing less physical activities, and causing short attention span are among common consequences of using technology. But there can be more severe outcomes such as autism, developmental delays, obesity, anxiety, or depression.
As Human-Computer Interaction researchers and designers it should be one of our main and urgent concerns to help parents overcome this issue without the need to completely separate the child from the digital world.

Brainstorm

We generated around 40 ideas, from which we filtered out 9. We ranked ideas regarding

  • Entrepreneurial value
  • Innovation
  • Best solution to previously defined problem

We kept our top 3 ideas and moved on to the next steps.

Interviews

We conducted several interviews with parents and their children separately and asked them about their life routines. We learned that kids' desire to use digital devices is strongly linked with the amount of time that parents spend with them. Kids are very willing to drop their digital devices when they have a chance to spend time with their parents. Also, if parents use their phones and computers all the time, this is what the kids also want to do.

Design Concept

"A shared space between the child and the parent to do activities together."
We named it Kiddo.
Kiddo is an application that gives kids and their parents' plenty of ideas of what to do together that are not just fun, but also educational. To put it in other words: Kiddo is an answer when the kid says "I'm bored" or when the parent wants to spend some time with the kid. The activities are divided into categories such as cooking, crafts, games, and science, and all these activities have something in common - they are all a bridge between the digital and the real world. For example, when the parents want to have 30 minutes of quality time with their child, they go to the app and select an activity to do together, like making a simple meal following the cooking instructions, singing a karaoke song together, camping in the living room, or making shopping list.
It also provides daily challenges to make the kid connected to at least one physical activity each day. Daily activities can be really simple like making origami, yet they are the rewarding system that helps us engage the kid.

Risks

The greatest risk that we took was something we found out from the interviews: there are two types of parents: one enjoys spending hours searching the web for activities, and the other one doesn't want to spend time searching the web nor wants to decide which activity is the best. Kiddo is only targeting the second group.

Conclusion

The problem that we tried to tackle affects all parents around the world and it’s growing as the digital world is expanding. Parents would love to have a stronger bond with their kids and have them improve their motor and social skills. As the first generation that has been surrounded by digital devices from birth hasn’t fully grown up yet, we don’t know the consequences that this digital world can have on their futures. We at Kiddo were trying to give our best to alleviate the side effects that all the technology might have on children.

Minesweeper Redesign

Desktop Game

Overview

Desktop game implemented in Java Swing (Not a wise choice for 2018, but that wasn't my decision to make.)
Solo project
Designed and implemented for Programming of Interactive Systems course (Paris Sud University - 2018)

Objectives

The objective was to design a game with the same concept as the old Minesweeper game (Meaning that there should be bombs hidden in cells and the goal is to uncover all the safe cells), but with a fresh user experience.

Storyboard

I introduced "lock" and "rotation" features and demonstrated their functionality in a storyboard.

Lock Feature

Locking a cell would defuse the bomb beneath the cell, and the numbers on the adjacent cells would update as a result. Users can reuse the lock feature after a certain amount of turns. All the other functionalities are disabled while using the lock.

Rotation Feature

Users can rotate adjacent cells around one arbitrary cell. It changes the location of bombs (if there are any), so the numbers will change. Of course, the user only can see the difference in already uncovered cells. The degree of rotation depends on the speed and strength of user input. The total number of rotations is limited in a game.

Implementation

Here is a screenshot of the final version in which I replaced the numeric system (to show the number of neighbor cells containing bombs) with a color-coding system.

Marionette

Digital Fabrication Project

Overview

Wooden Marionette controlled by an Android app
Using an Arduino board, Step Motors and Bluetooth module
Solo project
Designed and implemented for Digital Fabrication course (Paris Sud University - 2018)

Design Concept

Social media are subconsciously affecting our way of thinking and behavior. Instagram, for instance, is defining beauty and happiness in the shapes of flawless skinny women and colorful healthy food having zero calories. This mannequin is a symbol of us all, blindly following meaningless trends.

Materials and Hardware

I designed the puppet and the structure to hold the hardware and threads using photoshop, and I used a laser cutter machine to cut all the parts out of a 3mm MDF.
An Arduino Uno board was used to control two step-motors, each connected to one hand of the puppet. I used a Bluetooth module to connect the board to the Android app.

Software

I developed an Android app with a UI similar to Instagram's Explore tab, with pictures of women posing for the camera. By pressing the "Follow" button above each picture, the puppet's hands would move to imitate the position of the woman's hands in the shown picture.

Alchemy

Augmented Reality Game

Overview

Augmented Reality Game, developed using Vuforia framework and Unity
Solo project
Designed and implemented for Mixed Reality and Tangible Interaction course (Paris Sud University - 2018)

Design Concept

Alchemy is an AR game based on the idea of so many popular games, such as the Alchemy android application. In this game, the player starts with the four elements of matter: Air, Water, Fire, and Earth, and creates new elements/objects by combining already existing ones.

The Game

I developed this game using the Vuforia framework, the most widely used platform for AR development, with support for most phones, tablets, and eyewear.
To play this game, you would need a device, a camera (an integrated webcam also works), and a set of physical markers. You start the game with four markers (each corresponding to one basic element of matter) and a 3D landscape picture on the device's screen. The landscape is semi-transparent at first, but as you progress in the game and find new elements, they become opaque and fill up the landscape. The goal is to create all the elements by trying out different combinations and completing your 3D scene.
As an interaction design practice, I tried to make the "element creation" as intuitive as possible. This was made possible using the proximity of the markers in a 3D space: E.g. if you take the markers for fire and water and bring them close enough, you would create the steam element. This new element then needs to be registered on a new marker with a unique QR code to be used in further combinations.
There are 40 unregistered markers at the start of the game and they have a virtual question mark 3D model attached. Once you register a newly created element to a marker (by simply bringing it close to the element), the question mark gets replaced by the new element's 3D model. Technically speaking, the most challenging part was to replace a marker's 3D model in real-time and with the right dimensions and properties.

Models

I made 3D low-poly models of elements and the landscape using Blender. It was my first attempt at 3D visual design and was very fun.

Solo project
Designed and implemented for my Bachelor thesis (HCI Lab, Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran - 2016)

Design Concept

A pervasive game is a game that goes beyond the bounds of one screen. Users can play it across multiple devices and the game would seep into the user's life, and even the boundaries of play and not-play would become illusive. We at the HCI lab were designing a generic framework for pervasive games that could be used for many different applications and scenarios.

Modules

  • Physical activity recognition module - Android
  • NFC reader module - Android
  • QRCode reader module - Android
  • Step counter module - Android Wear

Pop

Rehabilitation Exergame

Overview

Rehabilitation Exergame for Physically Impaired Children
Solo Project
Master Thesis (Technical University of Berlin - 2020 / 2021)

Overview

Children with physical impairments can benefit from easily accessible and engaging motor therapeutic tools in conjunction with their regular rehabilitation programs. Interactive rehabilitation games show promise in aiding physical impairments, as they could increase active participation in therapy while being less costly and requiring less effort in comparison to therapy sessions. In this project, I explored the design space for rehabilitation games and followed an iterative user-centered design approach to offer a solution in the form of an exergame. I developed the designed game and conducted subjective and objective user studies to evaluate the engagement of the game. The results showed a high engagement rate, as well as a significant difference in player engagement between variations of the game, where different types of challenges, namely motoric and cognitive, were included.

Game Concept

As a first step towards the concepting phase, a set of possible game mechanics and game genres for our exergame, as well as the physiotherapy exercises that should be developed into interactions with the game, were defined. After that, we did a brainstorming session focused on the interactions, followed by a revising session to adapt the raw ideas to one or more of the game mechanics/genres. Three refined ideas made it to the concepting phase, and after an extensive feedback round and an expert interview, one idea was chosen as the game concept: "Pop" the balloons.
This idea was simple yet very powerful in terms of applicable motoric and cognitive variations.

System Architecture

the flow of interaction starts with the player’s input which is their webcam video stream. The webcam captures the player’s hand/arm movements and passes it on to the image recognition module. The image recognition module then will process the stream as a sequence of images and will recognize hand-shaped objects and track their position in each image and then forward this data in text format to the game’s logic module. The game logic reacts to the data received from the image recognition module and provides visual and audio feedback to the player if necessary. If the received data triggers any kind of feedback it will be considered as an ”event”. The array of the events are saved at the end of each game session as a reference for game statistics and player’s performance. In parallel with this primary flow, there is a secondary flow of data which results in having the player’s webcam stream as the game background in the game’s interface.

To realize the functionality of each component and the communication amongst them, making use of several technologies and frameworks was unavoidable. In most cases, wiring several different frameworks harm the overall performance, yet, the final and presented setting outperformed several alternative solutions in which fewer frameworks came into play.

Experiment Design

An experiment was designed to measure the impact of challenge type on various engagement indicators, both subjectively and objectively. It was also interesting to explore interaction patterns qualitatively and extract research questions for further investigations. For subjective evaluation, a questionnaire was used, and the testing sessions were video recorded to be analyzed objectively after the test.

Results

Multiple variations of this exergame featuring different kinds of challenges were compared with each other, both subjectively and objectively. The results of these comparisons suggest that between motoric and cognitive challenges, motoric challenges positively impact the flow experience. The game, as a whole, showed promise in terms of engagement, and I believe that we will see more of similar attempts in rehabilitation games in the future.
