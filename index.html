Kiddo

Educational Application

Overview

Android application
Done by a team of 2
Designed and implemented for Project Design course in Paris Sud University - 2018

Objectives

When we were kids, we used to play outside all day and do all kinds of social and physical games with friends. We used to use our hands and make crafts, touch the soil in the garden, and have fun time helping mom set the table. We did not need expensive toys to feel like we are queens and kings of our fortress made out of pillows.
But things have changed. Technology’s impact on our daily lives cannot be denied. Kids nowadays learn how to use an iPad at an early age. Although an iPad could be used as an amazing educational tool, it can keep the child from developing other necessary skills like sensory motor skills and social interactions. As parents and tutors it is our responsibility to control the balance, rather than using digital devices as babysitters.
Statistics show that physical, psychological and behavior disorders are increased due to overuse of technology in developing children. Interfering with sleep and schoolwork, cutting into family time, causing less physical activities and causing short attention span are among common consequences of using technology. But there can be more severe outcomes such as autism, developmental delays, obesity, anxiety or depression.
As Human-Computer Interaction researchers and designers it should be one of our main and urgent concerns to help parents overcome this issue without the need to completely separate the child from digital world.

Solution Domain

From analyzing the problem domain we realized that our solution should provide some essential features in order to address most important aspects of the problem. This does not mean that a solution without these features won’t work. It means that these features could give us some hints to improve any idea that we develop during the iterative process of design. These simple yet so powerful features are the following:

  • Rewarding system: although it may seem so obvious, it can define the way we interest a child to achieve a goal. If we make a connection between digital world and real world in the way we reward, that draws the child into the reality and to her parents.
  • Building habits in children: repetitive tasks make habits. We can use technology potential to build good habits in children.
  • Making physical activities a requirement: lack of physical activity is the source of lots of problems and it should certainly be a part of our solution.
  • Parent Dashboard: there should exist a way to control all kinds of kids’ activities for their parents. This can also help the rewarding system to get connected to real world and build a common space for child and parents.

Brainstorm

We generated around 40 ideas, from which we filtered out 9. We ranked ideas regarding

  • Entrepreneurial value
  • Innovation
  • Best solution to previously defined problem

We kept top 3 ideas and moved on.

Interviews, Testing Ideas and... Failure

We conducted several interviews with parents and their children separately and asked them about their life routines. We learned that kids desire to use digital devices is strongly linked with the amount of time parents spend with them. Kids are very willing to drop their digital devices when they have chance to spend time with their parents. Also, if parents use their phones and computers all the time, this is what the kids also want to do.
We also had the idea of creating an interactive matt, where kids could play all the sidewalk games that we used to play e.g hopscotch etc. We decided to go out and test our idea on kids to see how they react to the opportunity to play such games. It was a failure, as kids either didn’t want to try at all or, if tried, got bored really easily and started to break the rules. We decided to drop our idea of interactive matt.

Back to Testing Ideas

We decided to conduct a second round of idea testing, this time with another idea. We created a video prototype to demonstrate what we had in mind. The video included basic features and we sent it out to several families and asked their opinion. Feedback were positive and we received some suggestions as well. So we decided to continue with this one.

Design Concept

"A shared space between the child and the parent to do activities together."
We named it Kiddo.
Kiddo is an application for parents and kids that gives them ideas what to do together that are fun, but also educational. To put it in another words: Kiddo is an answer when the kid says I'm bored or the parent wants to spend some time with the kid. The activities are divided into categories such as cooking, crafts, games and science and all these activities have something in common - they are all a bridge between digital and the real world. For example when the parents want to have 30 minutes quality time with their child, they go to the app and select an activity to do together, like making a simple meal following the cooking instructions, singing a karaoke song together, camping in the living room or making a shopping list by using the digital device.
It also provides daily challenges to make the kid connected to at least one physical activity each day. The activity can be really simple like an origami. Daily challenges and rewarding system helps us to make the app more engaging for the kids.

Risks

The greatest risk that we faced was something we found out from interviews: there are two types of parents. the ones that enjoy spending hours on searching the web for activities and the ones who don’t want to spend time on searching the web nor want to decide which one is the best. Kiddo is targeting only the second group.

Demo

Here is the demo of our final version. We used Andorid for developing the client and Firebase for backend.

Conclusion

The problem that we tried to tackle affects all the parents around the world and it’s growing as the digital world is expanding. Parents would love to have stronger bond with their kids and have them improve their motor and social skills, but the reality is that kids use technology for large amounts of time. As the first generation that has been surrounded by digital devices from the birth hasn’t fully grown up yet, we really don’t know the consequences that this digital world can have on their futures. We at Kiddo were trying to give our best to alleviate the side effects that all the technology might have on the children.

Minesweeper Redesign

Desktop Game

Overview

Desktop game implemented by Java Swing (Not a wise choice for 2018, but that wasn't my decision to make.)
One-person project
Designed and implemented for Programming of Interactive Systems course in Paris Sud University

Objectives

The objective was to design a game with the same core as the old Minesweeper game (Meaning that there should be bombs hidden in cells and the goal is to uncover all the safe cells), but with different features and user experience.

Storyboard

I introduced "lock" and "rotation" features and demonstrated them in the storyboard.

Lock Feature

Locking a cell will defuse the power of bomb under the cell. So the adjacent cells numbers will be updated. If the locked cell does not contain a bomb, lock will do nothing. User can reuse the lock after certain amount of time. All the other functionalities are disabled during using the lock.

Rotation Feature

User can rotate adjacent cells around one arbitrary cell. It might change the location of bombs so the numbers will be updated. Of course user only can see the difference in already uncovered cells. Degree of rotation depends on speed and strength of user input. Total number of rotations are limited in a game.

Implementation

Here is a screenshot of final version. In this version I replaced the number system (for showing neighbor cells containing bombs) with color coding system.

Marionette

Digital Fabrication Project

Overview

Wooden Marionette controlled by an Android app
Using an Arduino board, Step Motors and Bluetooth module
One-person project
Designed and implemented for Digital Fabrication course in Paris Sud University - 2018

Design Concept

Social media are subconsciously affecting our way of thinking and behavior. Instagram for instance is defining terms like beauty and happiness in shapes of flawless skinny women and colorful healthy food having zero calories. The mannequin is a symbol of us, who are blindly following meaningless behaviors.

Materials and Hardware

I designed the puppet and the structure to hold the hardware and threads using photoshop, and I used a laser cutter machine to cut all the parts out of a 3mm MDF.
An Arduino Uno board was used for controlling two step motors, each one connected to one hand of the puppet. I used a Bluetooth module to connect the board to an Android app.

Software

I developed an Android app with a UI similar to Instagram. There were pictures of girls posing for the camera in explore tab. By pressing the "Follow" button above each picture, puppet's hands would move to imitate the position of girl's hands in the picture.

Alchemy

Augmented Reality Game

Overview

Augmented Reality Game developed using Vuforia framework and Unity
One-person project
Designed and implemented for Mixed Reality and Tangible Interaction course in Paris Sud University - 2018

Design Concept

Alchemy is an AR game based on the idea of Alchemy android application. In this game user starts with 4 basic elements: Air, Water, Fire and Earth; and creates new elements (or objects) by combining those that are already created.

The Game

In the AR version which has been developed using Vuforia framework, I used square markers for elements, and the game is able to recognize 20 of them simultaneously. There is also a 3D picture of a landscape that includes some of the elements. Only elements and objects that are already created are opaque in the landscape. Other elements are semi-transparent. The goal is to make this picture complete by making all of the semi-transparent objects opaque.
There are 4 markers with pictures of 4 basic elements, and 40 markers that are randomly generated but unique images of black lines in white background. At the beginning 4 basic elements 3D models are attached to 4 markers with their pictures, and all of those 40 markers have question mark models attached to them. User can start with holding two of four basic elements markers and put them close enough to make a new element. For instance, fire and water will make steam element. This new element will appear on the right side of the screen, and user can attach it to a marker with question mark. From now on, that specific marker plays the role of the element that has just been created, which is steam in this example.

Models

I made 3D low-poly models of elements and the landscape using Blender. As the new elements will be attached to a random marker, user cannot recognize them by marker. So I made a table of all possible elements in alphabetic order and printed it to put elements' markers in their place and use them as the game continues. Name of elements were also included in the models to make it easier for user to find it in the table right away.

Challenges

Technically speaking, the most challenging part was to attach a 3D model to a marker in real time. For that matter, one needs to detach previous child of the marker and attach the new model as a new child. Also transform property of model should be set the same as marker.
Finishing the game takes about half an hour if you guess the combinations quickly, or use the cheat sheet. Otherwise, you need to try combining lots of elements together and see if they can actually create a new one, which will take some time.

Jomlak

Social Network Application

Overview

Iranian Social Network Android Application
Done by a team of 6
Product Owner: Rahnema Co. Tehran, Iran - 2015

The Story

Jomlak was already on the market and had around 1 million active users when I joined the team. The company had decided to redesign the client and upgrade server technology as well. Me and 2 other team members started to build an Android app from the scratch. I was mostly focused on user interactions and UX. I also was responsible for firts-time users onboarding. Later on, when we were halfway through updating the client, we started to program a RESTful Java API for the backend. We finished both client and server in a period of 6 months.
The company decided to shut down Jomlak in 2017.

Pervasive Game

Framework Modules

Overview

Modules to be used in a Pervasive Game
One-person project
Designed and implemented for my Bachelor thesis in HCI Lab, Sharif University of Technology, Tehran, Iran - 2016

Design Concept

A pervasive game is a game that goes beyond the bounds of one screen. User can play it across multiple devices and game would seep in to user's life and even the boundaries of play and not-play would become illusive. We at HCI lab were designing a framework for pervasive games, so that different scenarios could then be applied to it.

Modules

  • Physical activity recognition module - Android
  • NFC reader module - Android
  • QRCode reader module - Adnroid
  • Step counter module - Adnroid Wear
  • Jumping jack counter module - Adnroid Wear
